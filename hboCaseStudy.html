The Problem

Users of HBO Max have reported frustration when navigating episodes of a TV show after accessing said content through “Continue Watching”. After unexpectedly finding they’re unable to perform this task, they are forced to return to the homepage and search for said content using a different avenue provided by the application.

Proposed Solution

The proposed solution is to afford users direct access to an episode index after their chosen content has begun playing, as well as provide them a path to the previous/next episode that bypasses an episode index altogether if they choose.

User Research

User Interviews

5 HBO Max users were interviewed to better understand what pain points and/or blockers they experience while using the TV application.

Research Topic Questions Explored:

  • What kind(s) of viewers use the HBO Max?

  • What are the viewing habits of these viewers?

  • How easily are viewers able to access content they’re looking for?

  • How easily are viewers able to discover new content they find interesting?

  • Why do viewers choose HBO Max over competitors?

  • What features from competitors stand out that may be lacking in the design of HBO Max?

Define & Ideate

Problem Statement

The insights gained from user research led to the formation of a general problem statement:

Users of HBO Max have reported a lack in features which, as a consequence, make it difficult for viewers to quickly, and easily, access desired content, leading to confusion, frustration, and unnecessary stress.

Ideation

How might we design a feature and/or process in HBO Max that will help afford viewers quick and easy access to content they enjoy? To generate ideas on how to approach this problem, an excercise using the “I like, I wish, what if?” method was performed.

Feature Prioritization Matrix

Solutions generated in the ideation excercise were placed into a priority matrix for the purpose of narrowing the scope of the project. The goal was to focus on improving a single feature/process, which as a result, would have a high, positive impact on viewers, and at the same time, be simple enough to implement as a solution.

By using this method, it was determined that affording viewers access to an episode index within the “Continue Watching” pathway would be the best solution in terms of impact and complexity.

Current User Journey

To help illustrate the pain points viewers experience when attempting to access an episode index after selecting their desired content under “Continue Watching”, a user journey map showcasing the current task flow was constructed.

Competitor Analysis

Before moving onto the design stage of the process, a competitor analysis was performed to learn more about how other video-streaming services handle this problem.

Key Takeaways:

  • HBO Max is the only service in this comparison that doesn’t afford access to an episode index via “Continue Watching”

  • Content that plays immediately upon selection under “Continue Watching” will likely require an episode index to be accessible after the content begins playing, as can be seen by Netlix

  • No compared streaming service affords the ability to bypass an episode index when backtracking episodes

  • The data lends support to the notion that HBO Max has a minimalist design when looking at the # of interactable items

  • Why do viewers choose HBO Max over competitors?

  • Netflix has a clutter issue that HBO Max ought to avoid when adding a pathway to an episode index

Testing & Iteration

Usability Testing

Four usability tests were conducted remotely over Zoom. Participants were given a quick tutorial on how to navigate the prototype before being asked to performs tasks. They were instructed to use “WASD” on the keyboard to move up, left, down, and right.

Objective/Goals:

  • To discover if users naturally use the “Restart/Previous” and “Explore” buttons located in the pop-up menu

  • To identify any pain points users experience as they interact with the redesign.

Tasks that were tested:

  • Backtrack a single episode via “Continue Watching”

  • Backtrack multiple episodes via “Continue Watching”

  • Restart current episode being played

Analysis

An analysis was made on each task.

Task 1: Backtrack a single episode

  • 50% success rate

  • Some participants didn’t realize the “Previous” option in the load screen was a button

Task 2: Backtrack multiple episodes

  • 75% success rate

  • “Explore” wasn’t an obvious choice for some participants

Task 3: Restart episode

  • 100% success rate

  • Participants found the “Previous” text in the load screen to be confusingand thought they pressed the wrong button

Participant Feedback

Participants gave general feedback on the tasks and redesign

  • Most participants liked the having quick access to the previous episode, but they often made errors using the feature

  • It was suggested that an additional button be added to separate “Previous” and “Restart”, so that one button doesn’t serve two functions

  • “Explore” wasn’t an obvious selection for when it came to accessing more episodes

  • The pop-up menu fades away too quickly when not being used

Iterations

The following iterations were made based on testing and feedback:

  • An additional button was added to seperate “Previous” and “Restart”

  • Pop-menu will only fade after the viewer presses “up” on the contoller/remote

Reflection & Next Steps

Reflections

I'm NOT the User

My motivation for taking on HBO Max as a project was the result of my use of the application. While I initially considered myself a user, the more in-depth my involvement became in improving the design of the app, the less I was able to seperate myself from the design process. To keep my own biases at bay, it became increasingly important to no longer consider myself a user of the product, and instead, to defer to others who use the app to better understand their needs.

Drawing from a Representative Sample

There are limitations to consider when analyzing the methods used for user research conducted at the beginning of the project as well as the outcomes found during the usability testing. The participants used for both research and testing were conventiently generated by turning to those in my network. As a result, it could be argued that the findings from this project do not generalize to as large of a userbase as hoped. Further research and testing should be conducted to confirm the effectiveness of the proposed design solutions found in this case study.

Usability Testing: Translating to a Real-World Setting

When performing an experiment or testing an application, it’s important to consider the medium in which it’s being conducted, and how it might differ in a real-world setting. HBO Max in this project was redesigned for a TV app, something that utilizes a either game controller or a TV remote as a means for navigating the application. However, when it came to testing the redesign, it was perfomed remotely on a laptop using a keyboard. While it’s possible the outcome may not have been significantly impacted by this medium, it’s important to try and replicate a real-world setting as much as possible to minimize the degree in which these differences could impact the results.

Next Steps

Further Research & Testing

As mentioned before, further research and testing need to be performed to confirm whether the design solutions in this case study merit further development. To do this, steps should taken to ensure the sample of participants being considered adequetly represent the larger user base being targeted.
